The Guru Granth Sahib is the central religious text of Sikhism, believed to be the divine words of God to guide humanity. It is considered by Sikhs to be their eternal Guru or teacher. It contains the baani (words) and shabads (hymns) spoken and composed by the Sikh Gurus and bhagats (saints) from other religions who shared the same core message.

The standardized version of the Guru Granth Sahib is written over 1430 pages (angs). It is divided into sections containing the words of the Gurus and Bhagats in the form of poetry (baani). Music forms an integral part of Sikhi as it provides a color (raag) to the words, giving them important emotional and spiritual context.
